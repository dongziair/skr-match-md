Privacy Policy for SKR Match

Last Updated: April 24, 2026

SKR Match is a Solana-based match-3 puzzle game. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and how users can delete it.

Information We Collect

  • Public Solana wallet address.
  • Game progress, current level, inventory, and daily bonus timestamp.
  • Leaderboard display name, such as a resolved .skr domain or shortened wallet address.
  • Public on-chain transaction data for wallet-confirmed purchases.
  • Leaderboard reports submitted by users.

Information We Do Not Collect

  • Private keys, seed phrases, or wallet recovery phrases.
  • Real names, phone numbers, government IDs, payment card data, health data, biometric data, or precise location.

How We Use Data

We use this data to save game progress, manage in-game inventory, show the leaderboard, process user-initiated SKR token transfers, review reports, and prevent abuse.

Wallet Transactions

Paid power-ups require a Solana Mainnet transaction approved by the user's wallet. SKR Match shows the item, amount, receiver, network, and non-refundable nature of the purchase before requesting wallet approval.

Solana blockchain transactions are public and permanent. SKR Match cannot delete or modify public blockchain history.

User-Generated Content

Leaderboard names may come from user-controlled .skr domains. Users can report inappropriate leaderboard names and hide leaderboard entries inside the app.

Data Deletion

Users can delete their SKR Match server profile and local device data inside the app by opening Privacy & Safety and choosing Delete Wallet Data. The deletion request requires a wallet-signed message.

Deletion removes SKR Match profile data associated with the wallet, but does not remove public Solana blockchain transaction history.

Third-Party Services

SKR Match may use Solana wallets, Solana Mobile Wallet Adapter, RPC providers, Jupiter price APIs, and related Web3 infrastructure. These services may process public blockchain data or technical request data under their own policies.
